Francine Miller — Founder & Owner

Our Story

Fran's Flowers opened its doors on Valencia Street in the spring of 1995. Francine Miller, a lifelong San Francisco resident and passionate gardener, had one simple dream: to bring the beauty of flowers into everyday life for the people of the Mission District.

What started as a tiny corner shop with a single display case and a hand-painted sign has grown into a beloved neighborhood institution. Over three decades, Fran has arranged flowers for thousands of birthdays, weddings, anniversaries, and quiet Tuesday mornings when someone just needed a little color in their day.

"A flower doesn't think about competing with the flower next to it. It just blooms." — Francine Miller

From the beginning, Fran believed that a flower shop could be more than a place to buy bouquets. It could be a gathering spot, a source of comfort, and a force for good in the community. That's why since day one, ten percent of every sale has gone directly to organizations fighting homelessness across the Bay Area.

Today, Fran's Flowers remains a family-run shop. Her daughter Rosa joined the business in 2018, bringing fresh ideas while honoring the warmth and care that have always made the shop special. Together they source seasonal flowers locally whenever possible, supporting California growers and keeping arrangements fresh.

What We Stand For

  • Community First 10% of every sale funds Bay Area homelessness relief efforts.
  • Local & Seasonal We source from California growers whenever the season allows.
  • Personal Touch Every arrangement is made by hand with care, never rushed.
  • 30 Years of Trust Serving the Mission District since 1995 — neighbors helping neighbors.

Whether you're a first-time visitor or a customer we've known for twenty years, you'll always be welcomed like family. Stop by, say hello, and let us find the perfect flowers for whatever moment you're celebrating.
